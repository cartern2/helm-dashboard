Reserve Rule

Arrive with at least 1/3 fuel remaining

A practical Great Lakes rule is one third out, one third back or alternate, and one third reserve. Increase reserve for night, fog, strong wind, or limited fuel stops.

Weather Risk

Simple wind + wave + alert logic
Current Risk GREEN Light wind, low waves, good visibility, no marine alerts.

Risk Logic

  • Green: wind 10 kt or less, waves 1 ft or less, no storms or fog.
  • Yellow: wind 11-20 kt, waves 1-3 ft, reduced visibility, or building weather.
  • Red: wind over 20 kt, waves over 3 ft, thunderstorms, dense fog, or any advisory.

Go / No-Go Conditions

Adjust thresholds for vessel, crew, and route leg

Go

  • Forecast stable for full leg plus reserve.
  • Wind and waves inside vessel limits.
  • Visibility, fuel, crew, and daylight all acceptable.

Caution

  • Building wind, steep chop, storms nearby, or fog possible.
  • Alternate harbor identified and reachable.
  • Recheck forecast before departure and underway.

No-Go

  • Small Craft Advisory or worse for the leg.
  • Thunderstorms, dense fog, unsafe seas, or crew concern.
  • Mechanical, navigation, fuel, or communication issue unresolved.

Stop and Reassess

  • Turn back, divert, or hold position if wind, waves, fog, storms, fuel burn, crew condition, or mechanical behavior changes faster than expected.
  • Do not press a long open-water leg because a slip is reserved or the schedule is tight.
  • If any essential system is questionable, the decision is no-go until it is understood and fixed.
